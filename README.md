# Insurance Expenditure Analysis (Greece 2008–2020)

Analysis of household insurance spending patterns in Greece across different years, insurance types, geographic areas, and household groups. The project focuses on identifying trends and differences in insurance expenditure using SQL and Python.

## Dataset

The dataset includes household insurance expenditure data in Greece for the years 2008, 2014, and 2020.  
It provides information on:
- Insurance type (Life, Home, Health, Motor & Travel)
- Geographic area (Urban / Rural)
- Household spending groups
- Region and occupation (used in SQL analysis)

## Tools

- Excel for initial data preparation and dataset storage
- SQL for data exploration and aggregation
- Python (pandas, matplotlib) for data analysis and visualization

## Key Insights

**1. Overall trend**
- Insurance expenditure declined overall between 2008 and 2020, with a sharp drop from 2008 to 2014 and a more gradual decrease thereafter.

**2. Insurance types**
- **Health:** Decreased from 2008 to 2014 and then partially recovered by 2020.
- **Home and Life:** Gradual decline from 2008 to 2020.
- **Motor & Travel:** Largest spending category across all years, with a significant decrease from 2008 to 2020.

**3. Urban vs Rural (2020)**
- Rural households consistently spend more on insurance across all product types compared to urban households.
- The largest difference is observed in Motor & Travel and Health insurance.

**4. Health insurance spending (2020)**
- Health insurance expenditure increases strongly across higher household groups.
- Spending rises significantly above the €2,200 range, while remaining relatively low for lower groups.
- This suggests a strong positive relationship between household economic capacity and private health insurance spending.
